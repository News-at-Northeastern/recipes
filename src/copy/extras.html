  • Noodle Salad

    Ingredients for Salad:

    • 11 oz. pasta, fettuccini
    • 4 qt. water
    • 8 leaves of fresh basil
    • 1⁄4 cup scallions
    • 1 zucchini squash
    • 2/3 cup honey
    • 6 oz. Leftover or Rotisserie Chicken

    Ingredients for Sauce:

    • 1⁄2 cup Creamy Peanut Butter
    • 1⁄2 cup Water
    • 4 tsp Soy Sauce
    • 1/8 tsp Cayenne Pepper
    • 2 tsp Gochujang
    • 4 tsp Sweet Chili Sauce
    • 4 tbsp Lime Juice
    • 1⁄4 tsp Kosher Salt
    • 1⁄4 tsp Ground Black Pepper

    Thai Style Peanut Noodle Salad with Chicken

    Courtesy of Northeastern Dining

    Servings:

    1

    Time:

    35 minutes

    Directions:

    1. Wash and scrub all produce before prepping.

    2. Cook fettucine (leftover fettucine works great) to desired degree of doneness. Drain, and cool pasta. Set aside.

    3. Gather chicken meat from leftovers of pull from rotisserie chicken

    4. Make the peanut sauce. Combine the peanut butter, water, soy, cayenne, sesame oil, gochujang, lime juice, and chili sauce. Set aside

    5. Use a vegetable peeler to make strip of the zucchini into a large bowl.

    6. Next, cut the scallions into 1⁄4 inch slices (on the bias)

    7. Cut the basil (including stem) into small strips

    8. Chop the cilantro (including stems)

    9. Pull the carrot tops into small pieces

    10. Finish the salad: Combine chicken, cilantro, basil, zucchini, scallions, pasta, carrot tops, peanut, sauce salt and pepper in a mixing bowl. Taste and season with additional lime juice, salt and pepper if desired. Mix well.

    11. Place cold noodle salad on plate, and garnish with whole peanuts. Optional: extra cilantro, basil or carrot tops if desired

  • Hot Pocket

    Ingredients:

    • 16 slices White Bread
    • 16 slices Fresh Mozzarella
    • 8 slices Plum Tomato
    • 8 Basil Leaves
    • 1/4 cup Pesto
    • 8 tsp Balsamic Dressing
    • 2 cups Seasoned Flour
    • 3 Eggs
    • 2 Cups Panko Crumbs
    • 2 Egg Yolks
    • Black Pepper To Taste

    Homemade Caprese Hot Pocket

    Courtesy of Northeastern Dining

    Servings:

    8

    Time:

    40 minutes

    Directions:

    1. Place bread on cutting board and using a 4” cutter, cut out a 4” circle out of each slice.

    2. On one piece of bread place one slice of the fresh mozzarella. Sprinkle lightly with black pepper. Next place slice of tomato on top of the cheese and place basil leaf on top of the tomato. Place second slice of cheese on top of the basil leaf and lightly sprinkle a bit more black pepper. Carefully drizzle the balsamic dressing. Repeat these steps for 7 more of the circles. Lightly brush the edges with the egg yolk and place remaining bread circle on top and press lightly to seal the “hot pocket”.

    3. Place whole eggs in a small bowl and beat slightly. Place seasoned flour in a second bowl and place the panko bread crumbs into a third bowl. Set up your “breading station” so the flour is first, then the bowl of eggs and then the panko crumbs.

    4. Coat “pocket” in the seasoned flour shaking off any excess. Then dip into egg wash briefly allowing any excess to drip off then finally dredge into the panko crumbs.

    5. Pre-heat a table top fryer with oil to approx. 375 degrees. Place the pocket into one of the baskets and carefully lower into the hot oil. Continue to fry until golden brown and cooked through, approx. 3 minutes. Remove from oil and let cool slightly before serving.

  • Chicken Thigh

    Ingredients:

    • 6 chicken thighs, cleaned and trimmed
    • 3 cups rice, cooked to package instructions
    • 2 cups spinach
    • 8 oz. butternut squash or other winter squash, peeled, seeded, and diced
    • ¼ cup Parmesan or other cheese
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 2 tablespoons oil
    • 6 slices bacon

    Stuffed Chicken Thigh

    Courtesy of Northeastern Dining

    Servings:

    6

    Time:

    50 minutes

    Directions:

    1. Place thighs on cutting board and cover with plastic wrap. Using meat mallet or bottom of small heavy pan, lightly pound the thighs to flatten them. Set aside.

    2. Place oil in medium sized pan over medium high heat. When oil is heated, add squash until almost cooked through. Turn off heat and add spinach. Toss the mixture briefly so the residual heat will wilt the spinach. Cool mixture briefly and when cool enough to handle, add rice and season with salt and pepper.

    3. Place thighs on work surface and place approximately ½ cup of stuffing onto each thigh. Fold ends over to enclose stuffing and ending with the seam on the bottom. Wrap each thigh with bacon slice and arrange so the ends of the bacon slices are on the bottom.

    4. Place in a pre-heated 350-degree oven and bake until an internal temperature of 165-170 degrees has been achieved and bacon is crispy. Sprinkle with salt and pepper and enjoy.

    • 1 cup coconut flakes

    • ¼ cup flaxseeds or chia seeds

    Coconut Bites

    Courtesy of Northeastern Dining

    Time:

    30 minutes

    Directions:

    1. Mix ingredients together in a medium bowl and then roll into small, 1-inch balls Refrigerate in an airtight container.
